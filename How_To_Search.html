General Guidelines

This advanced and powerful search engine made it possible for you to search within the database in English and Chinese. You will soon discover how much searching time and effort you could save. To get started, make sure you read carefully the following general guideline for search string input.

(1) Search in Chinese:
In Hong Kong, names of both defendants & plaintiffs in civil litigations are usually in English rather than in Chinese. At present, less than 2% of the records is in Chinese. Should you wish to search in Chinese, please select “Chinese (Hong Kong) 中文(中國香港特別行政區)[zh-hk]” and “Big 5繁體中文” as your language and encoding respectively.

(2) Punctuation
Please skip all punctuation in your data input. For example, “Sample (H.K.) Ltd.” should be inputted as “Sample HK Ltd” except the following situations
i/: “apostrophe s” e.g. Cheung's Co Ltd
ii/: “dot com” e.g. Tom.com

(3) The prefix “The”
Just skip all “The” prefix in your data input.

(4) Abbreviations
Abbreviations are acceptable only for the following terms as input string equivalent to their full spellings. This system will indicate all records matched against your inputted abbreviations and the corresponding full spellings.

Abbreviations which will be recognized by the system:

 And/&; Building/Bldg; Company/Co; Corporation/Corp; Far East/FE; Hong Kong/HK; Industry/Ind; Incorporation/Inc; International/Intl; Kowloon/Kln; Limited/Ltd; Development/Dev; Factory/Fty; Investment/Inv; Saint/St; Import/I; Export/E; Manufacturing/Mfg; Manufactory/Mfy

Other adopted abbreviations:
AKA = Also know as
BTA = Both trading as
FKA = Formerly known as
FTA = Formerly trading as
TA = Trading as
WO = Winding up order (清盤令)
BO = Bankruptcy order / Adjudication order (破產令)
AO = Annulment order (撤銷清盤 / 破產令)

(5) Personal Name
For a personal name search, enter the surname first. For a foreigner’s name search in which you cannot identify which word is surname, please try more combinations. For example: Benson John David or David Benson John.

As aliases of defendants are not necessarily filed in writs records, just skip the aliases when input search string (e.g. “Chan Tai Man Tony” should be inputted as “Chan Tai Man”).

How to search

(1) Exact Name Search

1. Enter the full name of your target
 2. Select “Extract Phrase Search”
3. Click “Submit” button
4. The result will be displayed
5. Click printer icon to print out the result


(2) The Left Partial Phrase Search

1. Enter any number of the initial characters (minimum 2 words) of the name that you are going to check
2. Select “The Left Partial Match”
3. Click “Yes” button
4. Select the name that matches your search from a list of returned matches
5. Click “confirm” button
6. The result will be displayed
7.Click printer icon to print out the result

Special notes

Neither the defendant is liable nor the plaintiff is eligible to the claim until a judgement is reached.

The outcome of a civil litigation is difficult to trace as records of judgement and suit discontinuance are not disclosed to the public. Even a defendant has repaid the claimed amount in full, that particular litigation record will not be automatically deleted from the suit records.

In Hong Kong, the ID card no. of a defendant in a civil litigation or bankruptcy would not be released to the public. Defendants of similar spellings in different civil litigations may not be one & the same person.

Unlimited Companies may bear similar names as long as they have different registration numbers. Thus, corporate defendants(of such nature) with similar names appearing in different litigations may not necessarily be the same company.

As the pioneered service provider, DCM possesses the expertise and additional information sources to verify which of the retrieved defendants of similar spelling in different civil litigations are one & the same person/company.
